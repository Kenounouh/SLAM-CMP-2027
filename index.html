About the school

A school built as a bridge, not a syllabus

SLAM-CMP 2027 — the School on Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence for Many-body and Condensed Matter Physics — gathers advanced students and researchers at the University of Dschang for a week of lectures, hands-on labs, poster sessions and mentoring, moving from the foundations of quantum many-body physics through to neural quantum states, transformers, and physics-informed learning.

The school is organized under the Max Planck Society's "Bridging Minds" program, part of the Max Planck Africa initiative, connecting the Max Planck Institute for the Science of Light with the University of Dschang.

Tracks

Additional information

Two tracks run through the week — a hands-on machine learning track, and a state-of-the-art condensed matter physics lecture track.

Machine learning track

  • A Slack workspace will be created for all communication prior to and during the school.
  • Lecture materials will be shared by instructors in the Slack workspace ahead of the school — participants are expected to review these in advance.
  • Two setup sessions will be organized before the school to help participants configure their computers. Exact dates will be communicated to registered attendees.
  • All slides and supplementary materials will be provided to participants — progressively or at the school's conclusion — and uploaded to the school's GitHub repository.
  • Lecturers are asked to submit materials to Lavoisier Wah no later than 29 January 2027.

Special track — state-of-the-art CMP lectures

  • Speakers are requested to submit the title and abstract of their talk no later than 27 November 2026.
  • Participants are encouraged to interrupt speakers at any time with questions, for an interactive, dynamic learning environment.
  • All talks present cutting-edge, state-of-the-art research in condensed matter physics.
  • Each talk includes roughly 10 minutes of introductory content, with the remainder given to the advanced, colloquium-style presentation.
  • Slides must be submitted to Lavoisier Wah no later than the day of the talk, to be uploaded to the school's GitHub repository.
