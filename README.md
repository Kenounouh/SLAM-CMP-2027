# SLAM-CMP-2027: School on Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence for Many-body and Condensed Matter Physics 2027

## Where condensed matter physics meets machine intelligence

 <a href="https://kenounouh.github.io/SLAM-CMP-2027/" target="_blank">SLAM-CMP 2027</a> is a seven-day residential school bringing together lattices and neural networks, condensed matter physicists and machine-learning practitioners, Germany and Cameroon. <a href="https://kenounouh.github.io/SLAM-CMP-2027/" target="_blank">SLAM-CMP 2027</a> — the School on Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence for Many-body and Condensed Matter Physics — gathers advanced students and researchers at the University of Dschang for a week of lectures, hands-on labs, poster sessions and mentoring, moving from the foundations of quantum many-body physics through to neural quantum states, transformers, and physics-informed learning.

The school is organized under the Max Planck Society's **"Bridging Minds"** program, part of the Max Planck Africa initiative, connecting the Max Planck Institute for the Science of Light with the University of Dschang.

## About the School

The school has two main tracks:

### Machine Learning Track: 
- A Slack workspace will be created for all communication prior to and during the school.
- Lecture materials will be made available by the instructors in the Slack workspace ahead of the school. All participants are expected to review these materials in advance.
- Two setup sessions will be organized prior to the school to assist participants with computer configuration. The exact dates will be communicated to all registered attendees.
- All slides and supplementary materials presented during the lectures will be provided to participants—either progressively throughout the school or upon its conclusion—and will also be uploaded to the school's GitHub repository.
- All Lecturers are requested to submit to Lavoisier Wah the Lecture materials no later than January 29, 2027.

### Special Track: State‑of‑the‑Art Lectures on Condensed Matter Physics
- All speakers are requested to submit the title and abstract of their talk no later than November 27, 2026.
Participants are encouraged to interrupt speakers at any time to ask questions, fostering an interactive and dynamic learning environment.
- All talks are expected to present cutting‑edge, state‑of‑the‑art research in condensed matter physics.
- Each talk should include approximately 10 minutes of introductory content to ensure a clear understanding of the foundational concepts, with the remainder of the time dedicated to the advanced presentation (colloquium style).
- Slides for all talks must be submitted to Lavoisier Wah no later than the day of the talk, to be uploaded to the school's GitHub repository.

For any other information, visit the school's website: <a href="https://kenounouh.github.io/SLAM-CMP-2027/" target="_blank">SLAM-CMP 2027</a>
